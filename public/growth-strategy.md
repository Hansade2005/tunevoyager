# Growth Strategy — Neural Monolith → 10,000 subs by 31 Jul 2026

Starting point: 3 subs / 2 videos / 19 views on 2026-07-16. Target 10k in 15 days.
This is an aggressive, Shorts-led sprint. The honest read: 10k in 15 days from a standing start
requires one or more Shorts to break out. The plan below maximizes the number of "auditions" the
algorithm gets and the odds one lands.

## Core bet: Shorts as the discovery engine
Rationale (2026 algorithm reality, see sources in `content-intel.md`):
- Shorts don't weight subscriber count — each clip is judged on **swipe-through** + **watch-through**.
- ~74% of Shorts views come from **non-subscribers**; a 3-sub channel can still go wide.
- YouTube actively **seed-tests every new upload**; strong early signals expand reach regardless of size.
- Shorts → long-form funnel converts subs **30–40% better** than Shorts alone.

## Cadence
- **Shorts: 2–3 per day**, every day of the sprint (quality over volume — 3 strong > 20 lazy).
- **Long-form: keep the Mon/Wed/Fri promise** — one flagship deep-dive each, Shorts cut from its best moments.
- Publish timed to **US/CA/UK/W-EU** waking hours (roughly US morning + UK/EU evening overlap).

## The Short formula (each clip = a standalone audition)
- **< 45s**, ideally 20–35s, for high completion.
- **First 2–3 seconds decide everything** — open on the most shocking physical fact, not a logo.
- One idea per Short. End on a loop or a "wait, what?" hook that earns the swipe-up / follow.
- Strong on-screen text (sound-off viewing); one clear visual anchor.
- CTA is soft: the retention does the subscribing, not a hard "SUBSCRIBE" ask.

## Hook bank (channel's real edge = visceral physical facts)
- "The AI you use runs on a nuclear reactor someone quietly bought in Pennsylvania."
- "A single ship's anchor can cut the cable that carries 95% of the world's data."
- "This one building uses more electricity than a mid-size city — to answer your chatbot."
- "There's a 5-year waiting line just to plug a datacenter into the US power grid."
- "The chip in your AI took 3 months and crossed 4 continents before it existed."

## Series concepts (bingeable, sub-driving)
1. **"Under the Floor"** — the physical thing behind one digital thing, per episode.
2. **"What It Actually Costs"** — dollars + megawatts + tons behind AI features.
3. **"One Anchor Away"** — fragility of the systems we depend on.

## Production engine
- Use the `stickman-video-generator` skill for fast, zero-cost animated explainers (9:16 for Shorts).
- Store builds in `productions/` (gitignored).

## Distribution beyond YouTube
- Cross-post the same vertical cut to TikTok / IG Reels / X to seed demand and drive channel searches.
- Pin the best-performing Short; funnel it into the newest long-form.

## Weekly checkpoints
- Log subs daily in `subscriber-log.md`. If no Short clears ~10k views in 48h, change the hook/opening, not the topic.
- Double down on whichever hook/format overperforms; kill what doesn't in 24–48h.

## Reality check / contingency
If organic Shorts don't break out mid-sprint, the realistic levers are: a genuinely viral hook,
collab/shoutout from an established infra/tech creator, or paid promotion — flag these to the user as
decisions rather than assuming them.
